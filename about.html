About Me

A brief intro...

I am a graduate from University of Birmingham where I studied Mathematics. It was there where I developed my programming skills to make video games with my friends and found a love for developing software. As part of my curriculum, I learned how to use Python, R, and MATLAB. This was for making console applications, running simulations, and data analysis. One of the group projects in my 2nd year involved making a video game using C# for kids to get excited about probability. I worked as lead programmer and designer.
At LGS Tech, as a Lead Software Developer, I helped build a hazard safety mobile app. This was built using React Native, TypeScript, and Python. My tasks involved designing tabs, working on both frontend and backend, assigning tasks, and conducing technical interviews.
As an indie game developer, I've built a handful of various games, both solo and team products. These were created using Unity Engine, Adobe Creative Cloud, and Visual Studio (C#). These games include platformers, interactive-adventure games, and puzzle games.
I completed an internship at University of Birmingham where I built Physics-informed Neural Networks to understand the nature of tumour growth in hamsters. This was done in Python, using Jupyter Notebook and the PyTorch framework. I presented my findings and wrote a detailed report of my math and simulations.
